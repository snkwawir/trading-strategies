# S&P 500 Options Strategy Risk Analysis

## Overview
This project evaluates the profitability and downside exposure of multiple S&P 500 options trading strategies across changing market conditions using historical SPY market data, volatility estimates, and Treasury bill rates. The analysis focuses on option pricing behavior, spread profitability, hedging effectiveness, and comparative risk exposure across multiple derivatives strategies.

## Objectives
- Analyze the profitability of options trading strategies under changing market conditions
- Compare risk-adjusted performance across spread and hedging strategies
- Evaluate the impact of volatility and strike selection on profitability
- Model delta-hedging effectiveness and downside exposure
- Apply structured quantitative analysis to derivatives pricing workflows

## Tools & Technologies
- Financial Modeling
- Quantitative Risk Analysis
- Statistical Analysis
- Time Series Data
- Volatility Modeling
- Derivatives Analytics

## Key Features
- Modeled bull spreads, bear spreads, straddles, strangles, and covered call strategies
- Evaluated option payoffs using historical SPY pricing and volatility estimates
- Built analytical workflows for delta-hedging and risk exposure analysis
- Compared covered versus naked option positions under multiple market scenarios
- Assessed profitability sensitivity across strike prices and volatility environments

## Results
The analysis demonstrated that spread-based and delta-hedged strategies significantly reduced downside exposure while maintaining strong profitability during bullish market conditions. The project highlighted the importance of volatility assessment and dynamic risk management in modern derivatives trading workflows.
